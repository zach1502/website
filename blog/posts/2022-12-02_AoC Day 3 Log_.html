Sets | AoC Day 3

Posted on December 2st, 2022

The early questions are pretty easy, and it helps refresh my memory on the C++ standard library.

Today's question was about finding the common character in the 2 halves of a string and assign a value to it and then sum all the values.

My first instinct approach was to read in a line, split it in half, find the common character in a naive way, and then add the common character's value to the total. This question wasn't hard at all I thought I was done 4 minutes in but tragically, my approach had a flaw. Oddly enough, this approach did not work. I was getting the wrong answer. I debugged and debugged, but I couldn't find the problem. To the point I got two 5 minute submission cooldowns! I then remembered sets. Sets are a data structure that only contains unique elements. I then realized that I could use sets to find the common character. Which I did, and it worked magically. I still do not know why my initial approach didn't work, I could've reached the global leaderboard but I'm glad I found a solution.

Part 2's twist was that you will now find the common character in a group of 3 strings. This isn't a hard modification to make with my code. I just had to add a third set the set intersection and adjust the characters added to each set.

So to sum up the results.

Part 1 took sluggish 30:20 minutes to complete thus getting me rank 10879. Part 2 took 33:28 minutes to complete thus getting me rank 7466.

I am now 14th on the Computer Science Student Society (CSSS) leaderboard, down from 11th. There are now 103 people in leaderboard, It's an okay result. At the very least I am still in the top 10% of the leaderboard. Which is my realistic goal. My ultimate goal of being number 1 is still a long way off, the people ahead of me are all experienced competitive programmers. I'm glad that I can somewhat keep pace with them.
